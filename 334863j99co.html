Step 1: How to conduct a meta analysis

The first thing we will learn in this module is to conduct a meta analysis. A meta analysis is a synthesis of information where we obtain numerical data from individual studies and pool the estimates to arrive at a summary estimate. The following steps are involved in the process:
  1. We identify a specific problem to be solved and frame a PICO formatted search question
  2. We search the literature databases and identify journal articles that are published in peer-reviewed journals and other studies
  3. We abstract the study results
  4. We first test if the study results are so similar that they can be pooled together
  5. We then pool together the study results
  6. We test if the study results are influenced by studies that are large and preferentially published in high quality journals and we have not been able to include those studies that are small and have equivocal results
  7. We also test if there are subgroups that we must test for finding patterns of results. and analyse these data
Meta analysis is synthesis and pooling of results from primary studies to arrive at a summary estimate. Here, we pool or sum up the results from primary studies. We have learned so far that:
